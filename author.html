About the Author

Dr. Arnaldo Delli Carri PhD CEng MIMechE SFHEA

Academic Profile

Dr. Arnaldo Delli Carri is Curriculum Lead and Associate Professor in Stress and Dynamics in the School of Engineering at Coventry University, United Kingdom. He serves as Course Director for the BEng/MEng Mechanical Engineering degree programme, where he brings together his passion for teaching with his extensive research expertise in structural dynamics.

As a Chartered Engineer (CEng), member of the Institution of Mechanical Engineers (MIMechE), and Senior Fellow of the Higher Education Academy (SFHEA), Dr. Delli Carri demonstrates a commitment to both professional excellence and pedagogical innovation.

Research Expertise

Dr. Delli Carri is active in the research field of nonlinear structural dynamics, with expertise that spans multiple domains:

  • Nonlinear vibration analysis - Understanding how structures behave under complex dynamic conditions
  • Experimental modal analysis - Testing and validating theoretical models through rigorous experimentation
  • Nonlinear finite element formulations - Developing advanced computational methods for dynamic analysis
  • Model updating - Refining predictive models based on experimental data
  • Structural dynamics - Investigating how forces affect structures over time

Throughout his career, Dr. Delli Carri has worked with important industrial partners including AgustaWestland, Rolls-Royce, and GKN Aerospace on critical structural engineering projects, bringing real-world applications into the classroom.

Teaching Philosophy: Building Intuition

Understanding Through Exploration: Dr. Delli Carri's teaching approach begins with fundamental questions: "What is this concept really about? Why does it matter? How does it influence the world around us?"

His pedagogical approach centres on helping students develop intuition for complex engineering phenomena. Rather than simply presenting equations and theorems, he guides students through the conceptual landscape, explaining fundamental principles and documenting real-world applications and consequences.

This teaching philosophy culminates in the creation of interactive applets and simulations, where students can explore counterintuitive but elegant engineering solutions, developing deep understanding through hands-on experimentation.

The Vision Behind Explorable Explanations

Dr. Delli Carri recognized that many engineering students struggle not because they lack ability, but because traditional teaching methods don't always align with how humans naturally learn complex, abstract concepts. Mathematical formulations and static diagrams, while essential, often fail to convey the dynamic, interconnected nature of engineering phenomena.

Core Belief: "The best way to understand complex concepts is to interact with them."

This philosophy drives the development of each interactive lecture and simulation. Every applet is carefully designed to help students:

  • Visualise what equations actually mean in physical terms
  • Experiment with "what if" scenarios safely and instantly
  • Develop robust mental models through exploration
  • Master threshold concepts that unlock deeper understanding
  • Build confidence through hands-on experimentation

Commitment to Open Education

Beyond his role at Coventry University, Dr. Delli Carri is a proponent of open educational resources. The Explorable Explanations project is freely available to students, educators, and self-learners worldwide. This commitment reflects a belief that quality engineering education should not be limited by geography, institution, or financial resources.

Through this open-source project, Dr. Delli Carri invites collaboration from the global engineering education community, welcoming contributions that can enhance and extend these learning resources for future generations of engineers.
