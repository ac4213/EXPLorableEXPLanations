Introduction to Deformation Analysis

Structural members deform under load. Understanding these deformations is crucial for design, ensuring structures remain functional and safe under service conditions.

Core Concept: Different internal loads cause different deformation modes. This lecture focuses on axial extension/compression, shear deformation, bending curvature, and torsional twist.

1. Axial Deformation

Normal Stress: \(\sigma = \frac{P}{A}\)

Normal Strain: \(\varepsilon = \frac{\sigma}{E} = \frac{P}{EA}\)

Axial Elongation: \(\delta = \frac{PL}{EA}\)

For non-uniform members: \(\delta = \int_0^L \frac{P(x)}{E(x)A(x)}dx\)

2. Shear Deformation

Shear Stress: \(\tau = \frac{V}{A}\) (average)

Shear Strain: \(\gamma = \frac{\tau}{G} = \frac{V}{GA}\)

Shear Angle: Cross-sections slip relative to each other by angle \(\gamma\)

For beams: \(\delta_{shear} = \int \frac{kV^2}{GA}dx\) where \(k\) is shape factor

3. Bending Deformation

Curvature: \(\frac{1}{\rho} = \frac{M}{EI}\)

Bending Strain: \(\varepsilon = \frac{y}{\rho} = \frac{My}{EI}\)

Deflection: \(v = \int\int \frac{M}{EI}dx^2\)

Key Insight: Fibers on one side extend, fibers on opposite side compress, neutral axis maintains original length but curves

4. Torsional Deformation

Shear Stress: \(\tau = \frac{T\rho}{J}\)

Angle of Twist: \(\phi = \frac{TL}{GJ}\)

For circular shaft: \(J = \frac{\pi d^4}{32}\)

Key Insight: Twist angle increases linearly from fixed end to free end

Engineering Applications

1. Structural Design

  • Deflection limits: Service requirements (L/360 typical)
  • Vibration control: Stiffness affects natural frequency
  • Clearances: Movement accommodations in design

2. Machine Design

  • Shaft design: Torsional stiffness for power transmission
  • Precision equipment: Minimizing deformation
  • Bearing selection: Based on shaft deflection

3. Aerospace Structures

  • Wing deflection: Aerodynamic performance
  • Control surfaces: Twist and bending interaction
  • Fuselage: Pressurization effects

Practice Problems

Problem 1: Axial Deformation

A steel rod (E = 200 GPa, A = 500 mm²) of length 2 m carries a tensile load of 80 kN. Calculate the elongation.

Show Solution

\(\delta = \frac{PL}{EA} = \frac{80 \times 10^3 \times 2}{200 \times 10^9 \times 500 \times 10^{-6}} = 1.6 \text{ mm}\)

Problem 2: Torsional Twist

A solid circular shaft (d = 50mm, L = 1.5m, G = 80 GPa) transmits torque T = 2 kN·m. Find the angle of twist.

Show Solution

\(J = \frac{\pi d^4}{32} = \frac{\pi (0.05)^4}{32} = 6.136 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}^4\)

\(\phi = \frac{TL}{GJ} = \frac{2000 \times 1.5}{80 \times 10^9 \times 6.136 \times 10^{-7}} = 0.0611 \text{ rad} = 3.5°\)

Problem 3: Bending Deflection

A cantilever beam (L = 3m, EI = 10,000 kN·m²) has a tip load W = 20 kN. Find tip deflection.

Show Solution

For cantilever with tip load:

\(\delta = \frac{WL^3}{3EI} = \frac{20 \times 3^3}{3 \times 10000} = 0.018 \text{ m} = 18 \text{ mm}\)
